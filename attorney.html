Biography

Christopher W.
Dean, Esq.

Founder & Principal Attorney

Christopher W. Dean is the Founder and Principal Attorney of Dean Law, a boutique Philadelphia law firm dedicated to providing distinguished legal representation across real estate, civil litigation, and municipal law. He founded the firm with a clear vision: to deliver the same caliber of legal service typically associated with large institutions, but with the personal attention and accountability of a boutique practice.

Mr. Dean was educated at the California School of Law, where he developed a rigorous academic foundation in property law, civil procedure, and administrative governance. Following his legal education, he joined the Montgomery County District Attorney's Office as an Assistant District Attorney, where he built trial skills of the highest order, handling complex prosecutorial matters and developing an intimate understanding of evidence, courtroom strategy, and judicial procedure.

Thereafter, Mr. Dean served as legal counsel representing the City of Philadelphia in real estate and tax litigation matters — giving him rare, firsthand insight into how municipal governments approach property disputes, tax assessments, and regulatory enforcement. This perspective is invaluable when representing private clients in matters that involve or intersect with public entities.

Today, Mr. Dean channels that depth of experience — prosecutorial rigor, government-side insight, and deep knowledge of Philadelphia's legal and municipal landscape — into fighting for individuals, businesses, and institutions who demand sophisticated representation and expect measurable results.

Every client at Dean Law receives the direct attention of Mr. Dean, from initial consultation through the final resolution of their matter. This is not a firm where cases are handed off to junior associates. It is a practice built on personal accountability, precision, and an unwavering commitment to client outcomes.
